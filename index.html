Frontera

Rulebook

Frontera is a public Minecraft server. If you join, you've agreed to these. Most of it is common sense. Staff have the final call on anything ambiguous, so argue in your appeal, not in chat.

How punishments work

Most rules escalate on repeat offenses in the same category. First time is usually a warning or short mute, bans get longer from there. A few rules skip the escalator. Those will say so on the rule itself.

Chat warning → 1d mute → 3d mute → 7d mute Minor warning → 3d ban → 14d ban → 30d ban Moderate 7d → 30d → perm (appealable) Severe 14d → perm (appealable) Grievous perm. Sometimes no appeal.

Durations aren't fixed. Staff weigh intent, history, and how much damage was actually done.

Chat

01Spam#

Repeated messages, walls of caps, character floods, ping-spamming a staff member. If chat scrolls past everyone else because of you, you're spamming it.

Warning → 1d mute → 3d mute

02English in public chat#

Public channels are English so staff can actually moderate them. DM, party, faction chat, whatever you want there. The rule is only about channels everyone sees.

Warning → 1d mute

03Harassment#

Repeatedly targeting one player with unwanted attention, insults, or stalking. Building next to someone purely to bother them counts too.

Rape jokes, gore, "I hope your X gets Y", same rule. Severity decides the punishment.

Staff discretion

04Threats#

Real threats to grief, dox, DDoS or hurt someone. Threatening to burn down a rival faction's castle in the next war is fine, that's the game. The rule is for stuff you actually mean outside of it.

Staff discretion

05Impersonating staff#

Pretending to be a mod or admin. Fake name colors, fake "you've been warned" messages, telling someone you can ban them. Doesn't matter if it was a joke.

7d ban → perm

06Advertising other servers#

IPs, domains, invite links for other Minecraft servers. DM advertising is worse than chat. Expect a longer ban if you do it that way.

Staff discretion

07Skins, capes, names#

Your skin, cape, and username show up over your head whether other players want to see them or not. We get reports about them.

Explicit or sexual skins, permanent ban, no warning. Hate-symbol skins (swastikas, slurs in your IGN, etc.) get a warning or ban depending on how blatant it is. For borderline stuff, we ask you to change before we ban.

Explicit: perm. Hate symbol: warning → ban (staff discretion)

Sexual content

08NSFW builds#

Sexual builds, pixel art, signs. We tear them down on sight, and the ban scales with how graphic it was. A cobblestone dick is a warning. Anything actually pornographic gets the long ban.

Build removed + warning → 7d ban → perm

09Sharing explicit media#

Posting or linking graphic sexual content anywhere on Frontera. How far the ban goes depends on what it was and who had to see it, up to permanent for the worst of it.

Staff discretion

10Erotic roleplay#

Public chat, voice, signs, no. Permanent ban.

Builds, theft & griefing

11Theft#

Items inside a structure are protected. A house, chest room, fenced enclosure, anything walled off counts. Taking them is theft.

An item dropped on an open path with nothing around it isn't. If you find a loose stack of cobble in the middle of a field, it's yours.

When you're not sure: don't take it. Staff judge by context.

Return items + warning → 3d ban

12Griefing#

Large-scale block destruction inside someone else's claim, or anything that ruins a build you don't own. The claim owner can do whatever they want to their own claim. Anyone else, no.

"Large-scale" is roughly several dozen blocks. Single misplaced blocks aren't a report.

Counts:

  • Tearing down buildings inside a claim without permission from the owner.
  • Removing structures you didn't personally build, even if the claim's unprotected.
  • Lavacasts. Just don't.
  • Builds made specifically to annoy a neighbour. Cobble pillars on their border, skytowers blocking their view.
  • Strip-mining or scraping resources out of someone else's claim in a way that wrecks the area.
  • Griefing the overworld road network. Roads belong to the server.
  • Mass destruction by fire, lava, or TNT outside of a declared, mutual war.

Repair + 7d ban → 14d ban → perm

13Lag machines#

Any contraption that tanks server TPS is banned. Doesn't matter if it's redstone, a flying machine, a cannon, or a mob farm. If it lags us, it's banned.

That covers orbital strike cannons, hopper monsters, massive flying machines, dupe-style cannons, and anything else with a known lag pattern. Entity cramming is server-capped at 30 per chunk, so cramming farms past that point won't even work.

Build removed + warning → 7d ban

14Auto farms#

Auto farms aren't allowed. An auto farm is any farm that runs without you making an active input on your keyboard or controller. That covers anything driven by detection, timers, or macros, and anything that kills mobs without you doing it, fall damage, other mobs, or any other non-player source.

What counts:

  • Farms that run completely on their own with no player interaction.
  • Farms that rely on a constant water flow, like large mob farms that push entities across multiple chunks.
  • Crop farms that only break the blocks above the base. Cacti, sugarcane, bamboo, kelp and the rest, you have to break the base block too, not just harvest the top.
  • Mob farms that auto-spawn or auto-kill animals and mobs. Armadillo farms fall here too, the entities get spawned in for you.

Automatic farms of these are flat out banned: iron golems, slimes, endermen, shulkers, creepers, skeletons, zombies, any skeleton or zombie variant, blazes, piglins, villagers.

A mob farm you actively stand at and kill mobs in is still fine. If it produces while you do nothing, it's an auto farm.

Farm removed + benefits stripped → 7d ban

PvP

15Kill harassment#

Killing the same player over and over to drive them off. No exact line. Staff look at how often, how long, whether the victim asked you to stop, and whether you're a faction at war or just one player being a dick. War, sieges, faction conflict are fine. Camping a new player's spawn for an hour isn't.

Warning → 3d ban → 7d ban

16Spawn trapping#

Trapping a player at their spawn so they can't move when they log in or respawn. Natural world spawn, bed spawn, /home, lands spawn, /spawn region. Boxing them in with blocks, lava, instant-kill setups, all count.

Warning → 7d ban

17TP trapping#

Using teleports to drop someone into a trap. /tpa requests sent to lure them somewhere lethal, lands spawn set to a kill box, /home points rigged. Any teleport mechanic used to deliver a player into a setup they can't get out of.

Warning → 7d ban

Raids

18Defender abuse#

Defenders can't use claim protection to trap raiders. No boxing them into 1x1s. No fully sealing the claim to lock raiders in or out. Trenches are fine as long as attackers have a way out somewhere. Walling up to the build limit just to block pearls isn't allowed, raiders need to be able to pearl in.

Exception: in-claim traps are fine if the trap design would still work outside of a claim. If it only catches raiders because they can't break blocks, that's abuse. If it would catch a player anywhere, it's a real trap.

Traps are allowed within a hard limit. A trap is fine as long as it deals no more than 3 hearts of damage and the player can get out of it within 30 seconds. Anything that does more damage than that, or holds someone longer, isn't a trap you're allowed to run.

Forced changes + warning → 7d ban

19Raids as casus belli#

Getting raided is a valid reason to declare war. If someone hits your claim and you want revenge, the right move is the war system. Don't retaliate with grief or alt-account raiding. War gives you the rules and the result.

Wars

War isn't live yet. It goes public within a month of launch at the latest, we're aiming for two weeks. Everything below is how it works once it's on.

War is a core part of Frontera, so it has to run clean alongside the rest of the server. The flow is reason check, ticket, fight, outcome. Here's each step.

War reason justification

Before any war goes anywhere, the attacking side clears two checks.

Casus belli (reason). Give a reason for your war. Write a paragraph describing why you're going to war and what you seek to get from it. Any casus belli that isn't descriptive, or isn't a fair reason, gets denied. A valid casus belli falls under one of these:

  • Territorial — border disputes, overlapping land claims, retaking formerly owned land.
  • Defensive — responding to raids or attacks, defending citizens from repeated attacks, protecting a vassal or protectorate, theft of valuable goods.
  • Diplomatic — violation of a treaty, violation of a peace agreement, breaking a non-aggression pact.
  • Punitive — punishing repeated raids, sabotage of infrastructure, or major theft or griefing; enforcing previously agreed terms.
  • Independence & sovereignty — protecting national sovereignty, succession or leadership disputes.
  • Prestige & influence — revenge for a previous war, responding to major diplomatic insults.

Evidence. Link and show evidence for your casus belli. Every statement you made in it has to be provably real.

Once the form's submitted, staff review it.

Tickets & review

Approved form, a staff member opens a war ticket with every opposing party in it. Staff weigh the claim value on both sides, build quality and quantity included, and both sides get a set window to argue their case.

Outcomes & roles

Defenders win. They can flip to attackers and go on the offensive. If they'd rather not, they get a war shield instead, nobody can declare war on them for 3 weeks. Raids are still permissible while the shield is up.

Attackers win. The claim drops under the attackers' nation claim, and cartographers mark it disputed but controlled by the attackers. The defenders then have to stay inside the attackers' nation claim for four weeks. Leave during that and the attackers can attack again, this time for permanent ownership of the land. If the attackers lose that follow-up war against the defenders for leaving, the defenders return to their own nation claim and get a 3 week war shield on top.

Closing a ticket

Staff close a war ticket when:

  • The defenders surrender the claim and the attackers win.
  • The defending claim ignores the ticket and tries to dodge. The ticket closes and the claim transfers to the attackers.
Claim value

If the attackers can't meet the claim value needed for the war, staff can ask them to pick another claim they already own instead. If they won't, the ticket gets closed.

20Casus belli#

Every war needs a valid casus belli from the categories in the war reason justification above. Write it as a descriptive paragraph, why you're going to war and what you want out of it, and back every statement in it with real evidence. Vague reasons and unbacked claims get the form denied. Getting raided is a valid CB on its own.

Without a valid CB you're not at war, you're just griefing, and rules 12 and 15 apply instead. No mid-war deals unless both sides agree to them separately.

21War conduct#

During a declared war, both sides can place and break water, webs, and cobblestone, including inside enemy claims. Other claim protection still applies. No breaking walls, no mining inside claims, no taking from chests.

Rule 18 (defender abuse) still applies during a war. You can't seal your claim or build 1x1 traps just because there's a war on. Same limits as always, a trap's only fine if it does no more than 3 hearts of damage and they can get out inside 30 seconds.

The post-war rules bind too. Defenders under a won claim stay inside the attackers' nation claim for the four weeks, and a war shield means no declarations against that claim until it runs out.

Forced changes + warning → 7d ban

22Insiding#

Using an alt account to infiltrate another nation's claim to grief, steal, or delete the claim is banned. Joining a nation specifically to steal items or grief, also banned.

Leaving the attackers' nation claim after losing a war isn't insiding either, that's covered by the war outcomes above. The attackers get their follow-up war for permanent ownership instead of a report.

Exception: organised rebellions are allowed, but only to take the land. A rebellion lets you unclaim chunks and reclaim them under the rebellion's own claim. That is the only thing it lets you do. No griefing, no stealing, no stripping defences. Those stay banned even in the middle of a rebellion.

To count as a real rebellion and not insiding, you need all of:

  • An organised rebellion with a Discord and real backing. Solo or spur-of-the-moment doesn't qualify.
  • Proof of organisation and funding. You're working with other people inside the nation, not winging it alone.
  • A valid reason. Overtaxing, bad management, dictatorship, that kind of thing.
  • At least 20% of the nation behind you.

If the nation leader reports you for insiding and you can't prove it was a legit, backed rebellion, you get banned. How long is staff discretion, based on the context and whatever you actually did.

Staff discretion. Theft or grief during a "rebellion" still falls under rules 11 and 12

23Mainland wars only#

You can't declare war on anyone using a claim that isn't on your mainland territory. Your mainland is where most of your builds and activity are. Planting an outpost across the map and using it to war the neighbours doesn't count, the war comes from your mainland or the form gets denied.

Cheating

24Hacked clients#

Fly, killaura, speed, reach, antiknockback. We need recorded proof before perm-banning, but a short ban can land on strong suspicion while we look at logs.

Benefits stripped + 7d ban → perm (proof required)

25Client mods#

Some client mods are allowed. Schematica printer for auto-building from schematics. Health indicators above mobs and players. Minimaps as long as they don't show entities or players (no ESP, no radar). Optifine and general performance mods are fine.

Anything not on this list, ask before you use it. Anything that gives you info or actions the vanilla client doesn't is treated as a hacked client (rule 24).

26X-ray#

Texture packs, mods or clients that highlight ores. Mining patterns don't lie. When your tunnel goes straight to every diamond on the chunk, that's the report.

Benefits stripped + 7d ban → perm (proof required)

27Autoclickers#

Software or hardware autoclickers, AFK attack/place macros. Drag-clicking and butterfly-clicking your own mouse is fine.

Warning → 7d ban → staff discretion

28Item duplication#

Any method, vanilla or plugin. Perm ban, item wipe across everything you've touched, faction inventory audit if you shared the loot.

29Bug abuse#

Knowingly exploiting a bug in the server or a plugin. If you find one, tell staff. Sitting on it and farming it is still abuse.

Staff discretion, usually rollback + ban scaled to damage

30Alt accounts#

Alts are fine. Using them to dodge a ban, pad faction member counts, or farm vote rewards is not.

Alt perm-banned + main's ban extended

Real-world

31Hate speech#

Slurs between friends in voice chat, we don't care. Using them to attack someone, or spamming them in public chat where everyone has to read it, that's a ban. Staff judge by context.

Warning → 7d ban → staff discretion

32Real-money trading#

Don't use real money to buy or sell anything in Frontera. No selling accounts, items, ranks, claims, or services for IRL funds. No buying them either.

Permanent ban on both parties + items wiped

33Doxxing#

Sharing or threatening to share someone's real address, phone number, real name, photos, workplace, school. Doesn't matter whether the info is accurate. The threat alone qualifies. Permanent ban, no appeal.

34DDoS & cyberattacks#

Threatening or attempting to attack the server, our infrastructure, or another player's connection. Permanent ban, no appeal.

35CSAM & paedophilia#

Any sexual content involving minors. Discussion, jokes, references, requests, same rule, same outcome whether or not an image is attached. Permanent ban. Reported to the relevant authorities.

36Real-world illegal activity#

Anything else that's a crime where you live and you're doing it through the server. Reported where appropriate.

37False accusations of real-world crimes#

Calling someone a pedophile, rapist, groomer, or pinning any other serious real-world crime on them with nothing to back it up. Saying it to win an argument or to smear someone is a ban, not a debate.

If you actually have proof of something serious, bring it to staff in a ticket. Don't run a witch hunt in public chat.

Staff discretion
